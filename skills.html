Product Management: An Introduction

IBM · Coursera

Defines what a product manager does and the skills and competencies needed to succeed, then walks through the end-to-end product lifecycle and the PM's role at each stage. Introduces core fundamentals: personas, value creation, and SWOT analysis, along with PM career paths and the industry-standard ProdBoK® body of knowledge, wrapped up with a hands-on final project applying the concepts in a simulated real-world setting. First course of the IBM Product Management Professional Certificate.

Product Management Essentials

University of Maryland · Coursera

Explains why product management is one of the fastest-growing, most sought-after roles and a common launch pad for future founders and CEOs, then breaks down the PM's position within an organization and the skills needed to work with teams and stakeholders. Covers how to establish, organize, and lead an innovative product team, choosing the right development methodology, structuring teams, and building a team charter, before moving into market development and commercialization: rethinking the 4 P's of marketing, customer development, and market segmentation and expansion. Part of the Product Ideation, Design, and Management Specialization (University of Maryland).

Supervised ML: Regression & Classification

DeepLearning.AI & Stanford · Coursera

Foundational course of the ML Specialization. Starts with the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning, then builds a linear regression model from the ground up: the cost function and gradient descent as the core optimization algorithm. Extends into multiple linear regression with vectorization in NumPy, feature scaling and engineering, and polynomial regression for non-linear data, before introducing logistic regression for binary classification: the sigmoid function, decision boundaries, and how regularization addresses overfitting.

Linear RegressionCost FunctionsGradient DescentFeature Scaling & EngineeringLogistic RegressionRegularization

Advanced Learning Algorithms

DeepLearning.AI & Stanford · Coursera

Moves from linear models into neural networks and tree-based methods. Covers how neural networks work and how to build one in TensorFlow, including coding a forward pass from scratch in Python, then goes deeper into training: activation functions beyond sigmoid, multiclass and multi-label classification via softmax, and the Adam optimizer. Shifts into practical ML development: evaluating models, the bias/variance tradeoff, error analysis, transfer learning, and responsible/ethical AI, and closes with decision trees, measuring purity via entropy and information gain, and tree ensembles like random forests and XGBoost.

Unsupervised Learning, Recommenders & RL

DeepLearning.AI & Stanford · Coursera

Capstone course of the ML Specialization. Covers two key unsupervised techniques: K-means clustering and anomaly detection using a Gaussian distribution, then moves into building recommender systems with collaborative and content-based filtering, plus a look at PCA for dimensionality reduction. Closes with an introduction to reinforcement learning: states, actions, rewards, the Bellman equation, and building a working Deep Q-Network on a sample problem like a lunar lander.

Generative AI: Introduction & Applications

IBM · Coursera

A foundational tour of generative AI. Covers the fundamentals of generative AI and how it differs from discriminative AI, tracing the evolution of large language models and generative capabilities across text, image, code, audio, and video. Databases & SQL for Data Science with Python

IBM · Coursera

End-to-end SQL for data professionals. Starts with core SQL statements: SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE, and filtering results with WHERE, COUNT, and DISTINCT, then introduces relational database concepts and creating/altering tables on MySQL. Moves into intermediate SQL: string patterns, sorting and grouping, sub-queries, and multi-table queries, before connecting to databases from Python using SQL magic and SQLite in a Jupyter Notebook. Includes a hands-on project querying real City of Chicago datasets, plus a bonus module on views, stored procedures, ACID transactions, and inner/outer joins.
