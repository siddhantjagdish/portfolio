My Story

The Journey

From curiosity to conviction — a look at the experiences, choices, and lessons that shaped who I am as a product person.

01
The Beginning

Lucknow to Bengaluru

I grew up in Lucknow, where I completed my schooling at St. Francis' College for Boys — one of the city's most respected institutions. Those years shaped a strong foundation in academics, discipline, and curiosity. I was always drawn to technology and the question of how things work — from software to systems to the products people use every day.

The decision to move to Bengaluru for university was the start of everything. It dropped me right into the heart of India's tech ecosystem, surrounded by builders, founders, and people who were genuinely passionate about creating things. That environment changed me.

I didn't just want to study Computer Science — I wanted to build things with it. That distinction shaped every decision I made in university.
02
University Years · 2021–2025

PES University & Everything I Built There

PES University gave me far more than a Computer Science degree. I graduated in 2025 with a B.Tech in CSE, but the real education happened outside the classroom — in clubs, hackathons, events, and projects that pushed me well beyond my comfort zone.

I founded HoPES, the official media club of PES University, and grew it into one of the most active and successful clubs on campus — a team of over 150 members delivering 30+ projects. Simultaneously, I led Ninaada, the Indian Cultural Club, and served as Event Head for Chords '23, the university's music festival with 2,000+ attendees.

Across my four years, I contributed to 9 clubs, societies, and labs, organized 10+ events and hackathons, and served as Special Person of Contact for the university's cultural festival attended by over 6,000 people. These weren't just line items on a resume — they were where I learned to lead, communicate, manage complexity, and build things people actually cared about.

On the technical side, I competed in hackathons consistently — finishing 1st in competitions with 150+ and 250+ participants, Top 4 among 250+, and Top 6 among 750+ participants. Each hackathon was a crash course in rapid product thinking, scoping under constraints, and shipping fast.

03
The Pivot

From Engineering to Product

The shift toward product management wasn't sudden — it was the result of consistently finding myself drawn to the why behind the code, not just the how. While my peers were excited about algorithms, I was more interested in whether what we were building actually solved a real problem for a real person.

Running HoPES, leading large events, and working in cross-functional university environments gave me an early taste of what product work actually feels like — identifying needs, building solutions, managing stakeholders, and measuring outcomes. By the time I reached my final year, product management felt less like a career choice and more like an inevitability.

My internship at SolutionBuggy Consulting as a GenAI Software Developer bridged the technical and product worlds — I built and deployed AI-driven backend systems from architecture to production. That experience deepened my respect for engineers and gave me the technical vocabulary that would make me a far better PM.

The best PMs I've admired weren't just strategic thinkers — they were deeply technical, deeply empathetic, and deeply obsessed with the problem. That's the PM I set out to become.
04
Professional Chapter · 2025–2026

Multiplier & Global Scale

Joining Multiplier Technologies as an Associate Product Manager was a defining chapter. Multiplier is a global employment platform that helps companies hire and pay talent across 150+ countries — the kind of infrastructure-level product that quietly powers global business.

I owned product initiatives across the invoicing and compliance domain — a high-stakes, high-complexity space where every decision touched real money, real regulations, and real people's livelihoods. My biggest initiative was streamlining the monthly invoicing workflow that processed $19M+ per month across 1,800+ invoices for 1,400+ companies. Using AI agents and workflow automation, we pushed the automation rate from ~92% to over 98%, saving 50+ hours of manual effort every single month.

I also built an AI-powered compliance monitoring system that tracked global policy changes across dozens of jurisdictions — reducing response time from days to seconds. Beyond automation, I worked on invoice anomaly detection, order form UX improvements, deposit refund automation, and expanding billing support for value-added services like insurance and legal advisory.

What Multiplier taught me most wasn't any single product skill — it was what it means to operate with urgency, rigor, and ownership in a fast-moving, globally distributed team.

Looking Ahead

What's Next

I'm actively looking for my next product role — ideally at the intersection of AI, automation, and meaningful business problems. I'm drawn to companies that are building at the frontier: teams where product managers are expected to be technically fluent, data-literate, and genuinely close to users.

Beyond PM work, I continue to build HMC Studios and explore new AI-driven product ideas. The entrepreneurship itch doesn't go away — and I think that founder mindset is exactly what makes a great product manager.

If you're building something ambitious and think there's a fit — I'd love to talk.

How I Work

Principles I Live By

Start With the Problem

Every feature, every decision begins with a clear understanding of the problem — not the solution. User pain is the only valid starting point.

Leverage Technology Ruthlessly

AI, automation, and engineering aren't just enablers — they're force multipliers. The best products today are built by PMs who understand what technology can do.

Ship, Learn, Repeat

Perfect is the enemy of shipped. I bias toward action, measure everything, and iterate fast. The market is always smarter than the whiteboard.

Earn Trust Through Craft

Cross-functional alignment comes from genuine relationships, deep domain knowledge, and consistency — not authority. Trust is earned one interaction at a time.
